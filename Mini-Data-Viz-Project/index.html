Introduction

Data is significant for the development and marketing strategies of companies. As companies and organizations continue to gather large amounts of data, the demand for skilled data professionals continues to increase. Nowadays, there are many job opportunities available for data science students in the United States.

However, the job titles in the field of data science are varied. Sometimes, even students majoring in data science cannot differentiate among the titles, such as data analyst, data scientist, and data engineer. This project aims to provide an entry-level introduction to job titles and analyze the current status of each job. The goal of this project is to offer a brief overview of job opportunities available to data science students in the USA.

About the Data

The raw dataset is the outcome of a web-crawling exercise that used the following search query terms: "data scientist," "data analyst," "neural networks," "big data and cloud computing," "machine learning," "reinforcement learning," "deep learning," "time series," "blockchain," and "natural language processing." There were roughly 85 searches, each yielding up to 10 job postings, for a total of around 850 jobs that are currently available online as of 04/14/2023. The searches are divided into two categories: nationwide and local searches for the United States as a whole and Washington D.C., respectively. It is worth noting that these searches are not mutually exclusive, and the raw dataset may contain duplicate job postings. After data combination, cleaning and deduplication, the final dataset contains 626 data points.

Shape of Final Data Set: 626 X 13
