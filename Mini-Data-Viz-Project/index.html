Introduction

Data is significant for the development and marketing strategies of companies. As companies and organizations continue to gather large amounts of data, the demand for skilled data professionals continues to increase. Nowadays, there are many job opportunities available for data science students in the United States.

However, the job titles in the field of data science are varied. Sometimes, even students majoring in data science cannot differentiate among the titles, such as data analyst, data scientist, and data engineer. This project aims to provide an entry-level introduction to job titles and analyze the current status of each job. The goal of this project is to offer a brief overview of job opportunities available to data science students in the USA.

About the Data

The raw dataset is the outcome of a web-crawling exercise that used the following search query terms: "data scientist," "data analyst," "neural networks," "big data and cloud computing," "machine learning," "reinforcement learning," "deep learning," "time series," "blockchain," and "natural language processing." There were roughly 85 searches, each yielding up to 10 job postings, for a total of around 850 jobs that are currently available online as of 04/14/2023. The searches are divided into two categories: nationwide and local searches for the United States as a whole and Washington D.C., respectively. It is worth noting that these searches are not mutually exclusive, and the raw dataset may contain duplicate job postings. After data combination, cleaning and deduplication, the final dataset contains 626 data points.

Shape of Final Data Set: 626 X 13

Brief Overview of Job Oppotunities in the U.S.

Aspiring job seekers are often eager to identify states with abundant employment opportunities and gain insights into job-specific details such as salary, work hours, and more. To aid in this search, here present a comprehensive map that highlights all the jobs in the final data set, with color-coded markers representing the job count in each state. Hovering over each marker provides a wealth of information on each job, helping job seekers make informed decisions about their career prospects.

Figure.2 - The map displays job opportunities in the U.S., with color-coded markers highlighting the number of job listings in each state. Each marker represents a single job in the dataset. Hovering over a marker or state provides detailed information for each data point.

Statistics Obtained from the Map

As per the map, there are a total of 537 full-time job openings available. However, with COVID-19 passing, the number of remote job positions is dwindling. Presently, there are only 130 remote job positions open. It's worth noting that the raw data set used for this analysis is derived from a nationwide search, as well as a local search in DC. Among the states with the highest number of job postings, California takes the lead with 93 job postings. Thus, for job seekers looking for data science positions, it is recommended to pay special attention to California-based companies. California offers a higher number of opportunities in the data science field than other states, which could be due to the presence of tech giants such as Google, Facebook, and Apple, among others. Therefore, job seekers looking for data science positions in California could benefit greatly from exploring opportunities with these companies.
